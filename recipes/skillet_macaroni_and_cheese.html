Skillet Macaroni and Cheese

Description:

Ditch the boxed stuff with this easy to make mac and cheese. With just a handful of ingredients it can be ready faster than boxed macaroni.

Macaroni and Cheese is often regarded as a comfort food for the not-well-to-do. While true, it's also a comfort food for just about anyone who thoroughly enjoys the texture of moist, squishy noodles and far too much cheese. You know who you are.

General Ingredients:

  • Noodles: Macaroni recipes call for "elbow" style noodles, but I suppose you could use any noodles you have on hand. Maybe hit up the grocery store real quick, and look for "hollow, semi-circle tubes", can't miss 'em.
  • Cheese: Colby Cheese tends to be the go to for this dish, but you can experiment with other soft/metlable cheeses. More cheese = less sad. Want some spice? Use pepper jack.
  • Cream: heavy cream will help with making the cheese more smooth and velvety. Remember that moist, squishiness earlier? This does all that. Maybe go easy on this.
  • Evaporated milk: I don't know, it's mac and cheese, this seemingly helps the dish taste less like your sadness and more like creamy happiness. If that's your thing.

Detailed Ingredients:

  • 6 ounces macaroni
  • water to cover
  • 1 (5 ounce) can evaporated milk
  • 1 ounce heavy cream
  • 6 ounces grated Colby cheese

Step by Step Instructions:

  1. Gather all your ingredients (and feelings).
  2. Place a medium skillet over medium-high heat. Pour in dry macaroni noodles. Pour in enough water to barely cover macaroni. Bring to a boil, stirring continuously.
  3. Continue stirring the macaroni until water is almost evaporated and pasta is tender yet firm to the bite, about 8 minutes. Pour in evaporated milk and cream. Add grated cheese and stir until cheese has melted and everything is well combined. Serve immediately.
